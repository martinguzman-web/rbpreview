Brand Identity Preview

The DFW roofer homeowners pick when they don't want to gamble.

A tightened positioning, a premium visual system, and a rewrite of every line on the site that still reads like stock roofer language. Not a rebrand — a sharpening. Built to make Roofboys feel like the operator it already is.

Proposed by Grwthly as part of the Location Growth Engine™ engagement. Every element below ties to a revenue leak identified in the Opportunity Brief.

01 · Positioning Thesis

Stop sounding like every other DFW roofer.

The current identity reads "Texas Roofers You Can Trust / Your Home Matters." Four hundred competitors say the same thing. The proposed position makes one operator-level promise and delivers it in visual language that feels more like a surgical specialist than a storm-chaser.

Before
"Texas Roofers You Can Trust. Your Home Matters."
Generic. Undifferentiated. Makes no promise. Matches every competitor.
After
"22-minute inspection. Same-day quote. In-house crews that don't subcontract — or we pay you $100."
Specific. Measurable. Risk-reversed. The wedge that already exists, finally said out loud.
02 · Proposed Palette

Copper over red.

Every competitor in DFW roofing paints their brand bright-red. It signals alarm, not craft. The proposed palette leads with slate and copper — weathered metal tones that signal expertise and premium work. Bright color is reserved for a single accent, used once per screen.

Slate
#14171C
Primary surface. Replaces bright red as the dominant color.
Graphite
#2A2F37
Secondary surface. Cards, sections.
Copper
#C8521C
Accent. One use per screen. CTAs, key numbers.
Bone
#F2EEE5
Light surface. Long-form content, docs.
Pewter
#8F969F
Metadata, captions, secondary text on dark.
Signal Mint
#A3D5C2
Live data only. Review scores, storm status, live chat.
03 · Typography

Three voices, one system.

The current site uses a single sans-serif doing every job. The proposed system uses three distinct typefaces — each pulled in for the role it does best. No fourth font. No exceptions.

Display · Inter 700
Same-day quote.
Operator headlines, hero, section titles. Tight tracking.
Editorial · Fraunces 500
Craft you can feel.
Long-form, content marketing, sub-heads. Warm, premium.
Utility · JetBrains Mono 500
REVENUE · AT · RISK  ·  LEAK 01  ·  $40K / MO
Labels, eyebrows, metadata, operator-stack language. Always uppercase, always spaced.
04 · Logo

Keep the logo. Change the system around it.

The existing Roofboys logo stays. It already carries equity with past customers, crews, and the DFW market. What changes is the environment the logo lives in — the palette, typography, spacing, and tagline lockup that surround it.

Existing Logo · On Slate
Roofboys
DFW · In-House Crews · Since 2011
Existing Logo · On Bone
Roofboys
DFW · In-House Crews · Since 2011
What we recommend

Keep the current Roofboys logo as the master mark. Standardize a tagline lockup below it — DFW · In-House Crews · Since 2011 — to use across site, email, jobsite signage, and crew apparel. No redraw, no redesign. Just a consistent supporting system.

02
Manufacturer certifications

03

05
